## 1. Purpose

This process performs a daily settlement reconciliation by retrieving a bank's settlement transactions, summing their amounts, and comparing the resulting total against an expected total supplied at the start of the run. If the difference (variance) falls within an acceptable tolerance, the process ends normally reporting the totals; if the variance falls outside tolerance, the process raises a case for investigation and throws a business exception to flag the discrepancy for follow-up.

Evidence: Start (start); Get Settlement Report (action); Compute Variance (calculation); Variance Within Tolerance? (decision); End (end)

## 6. Business rules

**BR-1.** The variance between the running total of transactions and the expected total must fall within +/- the configured tolerance for reconciliation to pass.

Evidence: Variance Within Tolerance? (decision)

**BR-2.** When the variance is outside tolerance, a Settlement Variance case must be created before the process reports failure.

Evidence: Create Variance Case (action)

**BR-3.** When the variance is outside tolerance, the process must raise a Business Exception rather than complete normally.

Evidence: Throw Variance Exception (exception)
